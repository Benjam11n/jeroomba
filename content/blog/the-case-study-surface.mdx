A project page should help a reader answer a few questions quickly:

- What was the problem?
- What constraints mattered?
- What decisions changed the outcome?
- What happened afterward?

That means the page does **not** need a huge narrative wrapper. It needs enough
structure to hold the facts.

## A durable outline

Use a predictable shape for every project:

### Context

What environment were you operating in, and what made the work important?

### Constraints

What limited the solution space: time, team size, legacy systems, or business
rules?

### Decisions

What choices did you make that were non-obvious or high leverage?

### Outcome

What changed after the work shipped?

When the format stays stable, readers can compare projects without fighting the
layout.
